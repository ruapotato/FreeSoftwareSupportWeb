About

Who's behind this, and why are we doing it?

Hi, I'm David

I've spent years working in tech, and one thing I've noticed is that regular people get left behind. When your computer breaks, your options are usually: pay someone a lot of money, spend hours lost in confusing forums, or just give up.

I started freesoftware.support because I think that's wrong. Everyone deserves access to tech support, whether you're a retiree trying to set up email or a student whose laptop is acting up before a deadline. The service is pay what you can. If you can give back, great. If you can't, you still get the same help.

The Philosophy

The name "free software" has a double meaning, and both are intentional:

  • Free as in cost - you will never be charged for help
  • Free as in freedom - I believe in open-source software and will recommend it where it makes sense

That said, I'm not here to lecture anyone. If you're running Windows and it works for you, great. I'll help you with that too. If you're curious about Linux or open-source alternatives, I'm happy to show you what's out there. No pressure either way.

The Recording Thing

Let's talk about the elephant in the room. Yes, calls are recorded and may end up on YouTube.

Here's why: creating these recordings turns each support call into something that can help thousands of people with the same problem. And the ad revenue and supporter contributions from the YouTube channel are what fund this whole operation. No recordings means no revenue means no free support.

I'm upfront about this because I think honesty is more important than making the pitch sound perfect. You'll always know you're being recorded. If that's not for you, no hard feelings.

Open Source All the Way Down

The tools I use are open source wherever possible:

  • RustDesk for remote support, open source and privacy-respecting
  • This website is a simple static site with no tracking, no cookies, no creepy analytics
  • I recommend free and open-source software to callers whenever there's a good option

Best-Effort Support

I want to be upfront: this is a best-effort service. I'll do everything I can to help, and I use every tool at my disposal including AI to work through problems. But some things are beyond what anyone can fix over a phone call, and I can't promise a solution every time.

While the risk of anything going wrong during a remote session is very small, I also can't guarantee the integrity of your system or data. By using this service, you accept that freesoftware.support is not responsible for any damages, data loss, or system issues that may occur. This is free help offered in good faith, not a contract.
